Mark & Sweep —
How Memory Learned to Forget

McCarthy's solution to the accumulation problem. Two passes over memory: one to trace what's reachable, one to reclaim everything else. The first garbage collector ever written.

The algorithm runs in two phases and touches every cell exactly twice. It requires no cooperation from the program, no reference counts, no programmer annotations. Reachability is the only criterion for survival. Every cell that cannot be reached from the roots, by any chain of pointers, is automatically returned to the free list.

— The first garbage collector  ·  John McCarthy, LISP 1.5, 1960
